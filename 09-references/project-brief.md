## Project Background

Smart Homes can enable people to reduce their carbon footprints and save money in the process. They can support busy families as well as enabling independent living for those who might otherwise need care. Domestic smart systems are a growing set of industries and are based around a range of different platforms.

## Project Brief

An architectural firm are designing a small housing estate consisting of:

- **6x 4-bedroom family homes**
- **Sheltered accommodation** with a warden for **14 elderly people**, who may have disabilities
- **8x 2-bedroom affordable starter homes**

All of the homes will have solar panels as standard and the firm want to incorporate as many smart features and devices as is feasible. They have engaged you to evaluate and identify the most appropriate technologies and to cost them accordingly.

## Requirements

A report and overall design for each of the smart home types, with full costing of parts only.

## Assumptions

- The task is set in the **UK** and all costing needs to be in **pounds sterling**
- You should assume that the site has access to **high bandwidth broadband**

## Task Suggestions

### Types of Smart Home and Smart Devices

You should look at: heating systems / home protection / support for independent living / solar systems / smart domestic goods / Google, Apple and Amazon devices / Energy monitoring systems and smart meters etc.

### Smart Home Features that would be appropriate

There are smart features that might help an elderly person should they fall, that might not be appropriate in a family home.

### Sensors and their Uses in a Smart Home

What sensors are available and how might they be used to support the inhabitants of the homes?

### Protocols Used

What protocols are used by the smart system?

### Integration of Different Smart Devices

This should explore if solar panel system information is passed to devices in the home to switch on when power is free (because the sun is shining) for example.

## Key Stakeholders

- The Architects
- The families who will live in these homes
- The owners of the care home
- Occupants of the care home

## Project Considerations

Your report will include at least the following in appropriate sections:

- An Introduction to Smart technologies
- Types of systems and devices/software/platforms/protocols/standards used
- Your recommendation for each type of housing with a rationale (this will need detailed diagrams)
- Security (authentication, encryption...)
- Incident response and recovery plans
- You should indicate approximate costs for system features
- An appendix with data sheets and other relevant information
- The report must be produced in a professional manner with text justified, full referencing, good use of appropriate sources and a table of contents

## Organisation of Group Project Seminars

Students will need to have 2 meetings per week, one of which will/can be informal/virtual, the other will be formal.

1. During the seminar slot the seminar tutor will have a formal meeting with each group in turn
2. The seminar tutor's input is primarily monitoring. Students are responsible for preparing material in advance; running the meetings, and for the efficient use of time
3. Minutes are to be taken at each of these formal meetings
4. In the first 2 weeks/meetings the students should brainstorm, consider stakeholders, produce a PID, draft WBS, Gantt chart and 4 fields map, with all group members allotted tasks
5. The proposal and plan must be approved (signed off by the seminar tutor) before the group can proceed
6. In the formal weekly seminar meeting with each group, each student in the group will present a brief report on his/her individual activities over the preceding week in respect of the work/task(s) set, presenting results etc. (very late students will be counted as absent, slightly late students will be penalised)
7. These 'verbal reports' and reflective diary (see 10 below) will be assessed on individual specific contribution by the seminar tutor and count for **20% of the final mark**
8. Absence from any of the formal weekly meetings will significantly reduce the individual student marks
9. Each group meeting **must** terminate with the following student actions:
   - A minuted review & discussion/confirmation of the project status relative to the previously agreed plan
   - Minuted agreed formal changes to the plan if required
   - Minuted updated formal changes to WBS etc if required
   - Formal minuted allocation of the next weeks work/tasks for each student
10. All students must keep a personal reflective diary of all the group's activities. This must be shown to the seminar tutor each week
